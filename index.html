The Science

We can't read your brain.
Here's what we actually do.

No app can directly measure dopamine or serotonin — those chemicals live deep inside your brain's synaptic gaps. What we can measure are the physiological fingerprints they leave behind. SageSpace reads those fingerprints and infers your neurochemical state — then Sage responds to your actual biology, not just your words.

🫀
Signal 01
Heart Rate Variability
The tiny time gaps between heartbeats reveal your nervous system's state. Low HRV = low GABA = anxiety is building. High HRV = calm, regulated, resilient. Measurable right now via Apple Watch, Oura, or Fitbit.
→ Infers: GABA · Cortisol
🧠
Signal 02
Sleep Architecture
90% of your serotonin is synthesized during deep sleep. When you skip it, your serotonin baseline drops — measurably. We track total sleep, deep sleep %, and consistency. Three bad nights = detectable mood vulnerability window.
→ Infers: Serotonin · Dopamine
🩺
Signal 03
Voice Prosody Analysis
Your voice changes before your mind does. Speech rate slows, pitch flattens, and pauses lengthen when dopamine and serotonin drop. MIT and Oxford research shows voice alone can predict depressive episodes 3–5 days before onset.
→ Infers: Dopamine · Serotonin
🧬
Signal 04
Movement Patterns
Physical movement is the fastest natural dopamine trigger. Sedentary days consecutively reduce dopamine baseline by a measurable margin. We track steps, activity streaks, and sudden inactivity drops as early warning signals.
→ Infers: Dopamine · Cortisol
🫧
Signal 05
Behavioral Markers
Typing speed, response latency, session length, and check-in consistency are quiet signals. A teen who responds in 2 seconds normally but takes 40 seconds today has a measurable cognitive load shift — Sage notices that.
→ Infers: Dopamine · GABA
🧪
Our Commitment to Scientific Honesty
We infer. We never claim to measure directly.
SageSpace uses passive multimodal biometric inference to estimate neurochemical state. All neurochemical outputs are directional wellness indicators — not clinical diagnoses. No direct biomarker measurement is claimed until Phase III salivary testing. We will always be transparent about what we measure vs. what we infer.
